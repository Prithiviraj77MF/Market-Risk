**Market Risk Modeling & Regulatory VaR Framework**

Value at Risk (VaR), Conditional VaR (CVaR), Stress Testing & Backtesting
Designed and implemented an institutional-grade Market Risk framework to quantify portfolio risk using advanced Value at Risk (VaR) and Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR) methodologies in line with Basel III / FRTB principles.
Developed VaR models using:
Historical Simulation (HS-VaR) to capture non-parametric, fat-tailed risk distributions
Variance–Covariance (Parametric VaR) under multivariate normality assumptions
Monte Carlo Simulation VaR to model complex return dynamics, correlation structures, and non-linear risk profiles
Implemented Historical Simulation VaR to identify extreme downside scenarios, incorporating real market stress periods to improve model robustness and tail sensitivity.
Built a Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR / Expected Shortfall) model to quantify tail risk beyond the VaR threshold, providing a coherent and more conservative risk measure for extreme loss events.
Performed VaR backtesting using:
Basel Traffic Light Framework to assess model performance and capital adequacy
Kupiec Proportion of Failures (POF) Test to statistically validate VaR exceedances and identify model weaknesses
Designed and executed Stress Testing and Scenario Analysis, simulating severe but plausible market shocks to assess portfolio resilience under adverse conditions.
Calculated and validated Stressed Value at Risk (SVaR) using historical crisis periods, aligning with regulatory expectations for stressed market environments.
Applied statistical analysis and mean-variance techniques, including covariance estimation and volatility modeling, to enhance risk measurement accuracy.
Ensured model governance and validation readiness, focusing on transparency, explainability, and regulatory compliance under Basel III / FRTB Market Risk standards.
